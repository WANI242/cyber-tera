Choosing the right CCTV system for a small shop in Uganda

If you run a shop in Jinja or Kampala and you're thinking about CCTV, this is the plain-English guide we wish every client had read before calling us.

How many cameras do you actually need?

For a typical small shop (one entrance, a till, a stockroom, maybe a back door), four cameras is the sweet spot: one on the entrance, one over the till pointing at the customer side of the counter, one in the stockroom, and one outdoor camera covering the shopfront. Going above six cameras only makes sense if you have a multi-room layout or valuable stock in multiple locations.

Resist the temptation to over-buy. We regularly visit shops with 16-camera systems that the owner never actually checks because there's just too much footage. Fewer, better-placed cameras beat lots of cheap ones every time.

HD analog or IP: which is right for you?

HD analog (AHD/HD-TVI) is the cheaper, simpler option. Cameras connect to a DVR over coaxial cable. Good resolution (usually 2MP or 5MP), reliable, and a full 4-camera kit can land in the UGX 1.8M–2.5M range installed. This is what most small shops should get.

IP cameras connect over Ethernet (PoE) to a network video recorder. Higher resolutions (4K available), better remote-viewing performance, and easier to expand, but the kit and cabling cost 30–60% more. Worth it if you want 24/7 crisp facial-recognition-quality footage, or if you already have structured cabling in place.

Rule of thumb: single-location shop with a straightforward layout → HD analog. Multi-location or planning to grow → IP.

Storage: don't skip this

A 1TB hard drive in the recorder will hold roughly 14–21 days of footage for 4 cameras at 2MP. If your shop only has daytime activity, that's fine. If you want a full month of continuous recording — useful for insurance and for catching slow-moving issues like inventory shrinkage — go straight to 2TB. The cost difference is tiny, the peace of mind is real.

Power backup is not optional

Uganda's grid still has outages. A CCTV system that dies the moment the power goes off is not much use. Budget for a small UPS (600–1200VA) dedicated to the recorder and one or two priority cameras, and you'll keep recording through most outages long enough for the generator or battery to take over.

What should a small-shop install cost in Uganda (as of 2025)?

These are realistic ranges for a fully installed system, including cabling, recorder, hard drive, mobile-app setup, and a one-year warranty:

  • Basic 4-camera HD kit: UGX 1.8M – 2.5M
  • Better 4-camera HD kit (5MP cameras, 2TB storage, UPS): UGX 2.8M – 3.5M
  • 4-camera IP kit (PoE NVR, 4MP cameras, UPS): UGX 3.5M – 5M
  • 8-camera commercial IP kit: UGX 7M – 12M depending on camera choice

Anything substantially cheaper than these ranges usually means lower-grade components or cabling corners being cut. Ask to see the spec sheet before you commit.

Before you call any installer

Make a quick sketch of your shop with every door, window, and the till marked. Walk through what you actually want to know: "if something goes wrong, what footage do I need?" That answer drives the camera count and placement, not the other way around.
